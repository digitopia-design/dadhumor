## Strategic Approach

Dad Humor is being built as a passion-project-with-commercial-upside. The order of monetisation moves matters:

1. **Build audience first** (Phases 1-4) - no monetisation pressure, focus on retention/virality
2. **Soft monetisation** (Phase 6) - merchandise via existing Bantered infrastructure
3. **Premium tier** (Phase 7) - subscription for power users
4. **B2B revenue** (Phase 8+) - API, corporate packs
5. **Scale products** (Phase 9+) - subscription boxes, books, calendars

**Principle:** Never compromise the user experience for monetisation. Every revenue stream should feel natural, not forced.

## Tier 4: Premium Subscription (Phase 7)

### Dad Humor Premium - £2.99/month or £24/year
**Features:**
- No ads
- Early access to new jokes
- Unlimited Stash (free tier capped at 20)
- Custom collections with shareable URLs
- Premium Stache skins/hats
- HD share card downloads
- AI joke generator (50/day vs 5)
- Sound effect library access (premium pack)

**Pricing rationale:**
- Low enough to be impulse subscription
- Annual saves 33% (drives commitment)
- Stripe handles billing

**Conversion target:** 2% of MAU
**Year 1 target (assuming 50,000 MAU by month 6):** 1,000 subscribers × £2.99 × 6 months = £17,940

**Year 2+:** Scaling with audience.

## What NOT To Monetise

Some things stay free forever to protect brand love:

❌ **Reading jokes** - core product is always free
❌ **Stash (basic)** - up to 20 saves free, more is premium
❌ **Sharing** - never paywall sharing, that's the viral loop
❌ **Joke of the Day API (basic)** - free tier essential for distribution
❌ **WordPress plugin** - free, drives backlinks
❌ **Digital signage page** - free distribution channel

The brand is built on goodwill. Goodwill is the moat. Don't compromise it for short-term revenue.

## Anti-Patterns To Avoid

🚫 Pop-up ads on the main app
🚫 Aggressive upsell modals
🚫 Hidden paywalls (always be transparent about premium)
🚫 Affiliate spam in jokes
🚫 Sponsored jokes that aren't clearly marked
🚫 Email capture before users get value
🚫 Cookie banner that's hostile to deny

## Decision Framework

When evaluating new monetisation ideas, ask:

1. Does it add or extract value from the user?
2. Is it on-brand (does it feel like Dad Humor)?
3. Can existing infrastructure (Bantered) handle it?
4. What's the unit economics?
5. Does it scale, or is it manual forever?
6. Could it harm brand love if it goes wrong?

If 4+ are positive, proceed. If 2+ are negative, drop or rework.
